The Challenge

A Fragmented and Rapidly Evolving Regulatory Landscape

Government agencies face an unprecedented wave of AI regulation. Executive Order 14110 mandates safety and civil rights protections. OMB M-24-10 requires AI use inventories and risk management. NIST AI RMF provides a voluntary but increasingly referenced governance standard. And state legislatures--from Colorado to California to New York--are passing their own AI laws with unique compliance requirements.

Without a unified compliance framework, agencies waste resources chasing individual mandates, create redundant documentation, and still miss critical gaps. Sector regulators like HHS, FTC, EEOC, and CFPB are increasingly referencing NIST AI RMF principles in their enforcement expectations, creating a converging but complex compliance landscape.

Key Regulations Affecting Government AI:

  • EO 14110 -- Federal AI safety, security, and civil rights requirements
  • OMB M-24-10 -- AI use inventories, risk management, and impact assessments
  • NIST AI RMF -- Trustworthy AI risk management framework (Govern, Map, Measure, Manage)
  • AI Bill of Rights -- Blueprint for safe, non-discriminatory AI systems
  • State AI Laws -- Colorado AI Act, NYC LL144, California CCPA AI provisions
  • Sector Rules -- HHS, FTC, EEOC, CFPB, HUD AI-specific requirements
Our Approach

Unified Compliance Framework Methodology

A four-phase approach that consolidates fragmented requirements into a single, auditable compliance program aligned with NIST AI RMF.

Phase 1

Regulatory Mapping and Gap Analysis

Comprehensive inventory of all applicable AI regulations, standards, and guidance--federal, state, and sector-specific--mapped to your current compliance posture.

  • Multi-regulatory requirement matrix (EO 14110, OMB M-24-10, NIST AI RMF, state laws)
  • Current state compliance assessment across all AI systems
  • Gap identification with severity and urgency scoring
  • Regulatory overlap analysis to eliminate redundant efforts
Phase 2

Risk Framework Development

Build a structured AI risk management framework aligned with the four NIST AI RMF core functions: Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage.

  • AI risk taxonomy covering safety, legal, operational, ethical, reputational, and security risks
  • Risk tiering methodology with approval workflows per tier
  • Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA) process and templates
  • NIST AI RMF function-level implementation plans
Phase 3

Controls Implementation and Documentation

Deploy compliance controls, create audit-ready documentation, and establish the governance processes needed to demonstrate ongoing compliance.

  • AI use case inventory system (OMB-compliant public reporting)
  • Compliance control library mapped to each regulatory requirement
  • Documentation standards for risk assessments, impact assessments, and approvals
  • Audit trail and evidence management procedures
Phase 4

Continuous Monitoring and Regulatory Intelligence

Establish ongoing compliance monitoring, regulatory change tracking, and periodic reassessment to keep pace with evolving requirements.

  • Compliance dashboard with real-time status across all regulatory domains
  • Regulatory intelligence service tracking new and proposed AI laws
  • Quarterly compliance review cadence with remediation tracking
  • Audit preparation support and GAO/IG readiness assessments
