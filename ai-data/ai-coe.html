The Challenge

Fragmented AI Efforts Cannot Scale

Most government agencies attempting AI find themselves in the same trap: multiple departments running disconnected pilots, duplicating vendor contracts, applying inconsistent governance, and struggling to share lessons learned. Deloitte's 2026 research confirms that agencies struggle to move beyond small-scale pilots into enterprise-wide adoption.

An AI Center of Excellence solves this by providing centralized strategy, shared expertise, consistent governance, and reusable assets. The world's leading consulting firms -- including Slalom, Accenture, and Deloitte -- have established their own AI CoEs, and they recommend the same model for their government clients. We help you build yours.

Without an AI CoE:

  • Departments duplicate AI investments and vendor contracts
  • No consistent governance or responsible AI standards
  • Lessons from one project never reach other teams
  • Scarce AI talent is spread too thin across the organization
Our Approach

Four-Phase CoE Development

A proven methodology to design, staff, launch, and scale a government AI Center of Excellence -- from initial assessment through full operational maturity.

Phase 1

Assess & Design

Evaluate your current AI landscape, organizational structure, and strategic goals to design a CoE model that fits. We assess existing AI projects, identify gaps in governance, and benchmark against leading government and commercial CoEs to define the right scope, structure, and operating model.

  • Current state assessment of AI projects and capabilities
  • Stakeholder interviews and organizational analysis
  • Operating model selection (centralized, federated, or hybrid)
  • CoE charter, scope definition, and success metrics
  • Budget model and funding strategy
Phase 2

Staff & Structure

Define the roles, skills, and organizational structure your CoE needs to succeed. This includes recruiting strategy for hard-to-find AI talent, position descriptions aligned to federal classification standards, and an onboarding program that gets your team productive fast.

  • Role definitions and competency models
  • Talent acquisition strategy and position descriptions
  • Organizational reporting structure and governance board design
  • Vendor and partner ecosystem strategy
  • Onboarding and team development program
Phase 3

Build Capabilities

Develop the processes, tools, governance frameworks, and training programs that make the CoE operational. This phase creates the reusable assets -- delivery methodology, evaluation templates, risk frameworks, and knowledge base -- that enable the CoE to serve the entire organization.

  • AI delivery methodology and project lifecycle framework
  • Governance policies, ethics review board, and risk assessment processes
  • Reusable templates: business cases, model cards, deployment playbooks
  • AI knowledge base and community of practice launch
  • Training curriculum for CoE staff and agency partners
Phase 4

Launch & Scale

Execute initial high-visibility projects that prove the CoE's value, then systematically expand reach across the organization. We stay engaged through the first wave of projects to coach your team, refine processes, and ensure the CoE delivers demonstrable ROI that justifies continued investment.

  • First wave project selection and execution support
  • Value demonstration and ROI reporting to leadership
  • Expansion planning to additional departments and use cases
  • Maturity assessment and continuous improvement roadmap
  • Transition to independent operations with ongoing advisory

Why a CoE Matters

A CoE Turns Scattered Experiments into Enterprise Capability

Slalom's 2026 AI research found that the ambition-execution gap is widening: organizations that invest in AI without centralizing expertise and governance consistently underperform those with structured CoE models. For government agencies, where public accountability demands consistency, the case is even stronger.

  • Eliminates duplication -- one team, one platform strategy, one set of vendor relationships instead of fragmented departmental efforts
  • Ensures consistency -- every AI initiative follows the same governance, ethics review, and quality standards
  • Accelerates delivery -- reusable assets, shared expertise, and standardized processes mean each project is faster than the last
  • Attracts and retains talent -- AI professionals want to work in a center with peers, mentorship, and challenging projects
