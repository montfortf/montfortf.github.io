When to Fine-Tune

Fine-Tuning vs. Prompting vs. RAG

Prompt Engineering

Use when you need flexibility and have diverse tasks. Good for exploration and quick iteration.

Best for:

General tasks, prototyping, low volume

RAG

Use when you need AI to access specific knowledge that changes over time.

Best for:

Q&A over documents, dynamic content

Fine-Tuning

Use when you need consistent behavior, specific output formats, or domain expertise.

Best for:

High-volume, specialized tasks

Our Process

Fine-Tuning Methodology

1

Requirements & Feasibility

Define the task, assess data availability, and determine if fine-tuning is the right approach vs. prompting or RAG.

2

Data Preparation

Collect, clean, and format training data. Create high-quality examples that represent the desired behavior.

3

Base Model Selection

Choose the appropriate base model based on task requirements, cost, latency, and deployment constraints.

4

Training & Iteration

Fine-tune the model, evaluate on held-out data, and iterate on training data and hyperparameters.

5

Evaluation & Testing

Rigorous evaluation on diverse test cases, edge cases, and adversarial examples. Safety and bias testing.

6

Deployment & Monitoring

Deploy to production with monitoring for quality, drift, and performance. Plan for periodic retraining.

Techniques

Fine-Tuning Approaches

Full Fine-Tuning

Update all model parameters. Maximum customization but requires significant compute and data.

High Resource

LoRA / QLoRA

Low-rank adaptation updates a small subset of parameters. Efficient and effective for most tasks.

Recommended

Instruction Tuning

Train on instruction-response pairs to improve the model's ability to follow specific directions.
