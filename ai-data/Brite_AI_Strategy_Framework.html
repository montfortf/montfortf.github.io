Use Case Identification & Scoring

Brainstorm potential AI applications, then score each one objectively. The goal is a prioritized portfolio of 10–15 opportunities ranked by impact and feasibility.

Scoring Criteria

Rate each use case from 1–5 across four dimensions. Multiply each score by the weight to get a composite priority score.

Step 05 — 3-Year Phased Roadmap
Step 05

3-Year Phased Roadmap

Assign your top-scored use cases into three phases. Quick wins build momentum and demonstrate value. Mid-term initiatives expand capability. Transformation projects deliver lasting change.

Phase 1 — Quick Wins
Foundation
Months 0–6
  • Deploy 1–3 highest-scored, lowest-risk use cases
  • Establish AI governance framework
  • Launch data quality remediation
  • Begin workforce AI literacy training
  • Demonstrate ROI to leadership
Phase 2 — Expansion
Scale
Months 6–18
  • Roll out 3–5 mid-complexity use cases
  • Build AI Center of Excellence
  • Integrate AI into core workflows
  • Expand data platform capabilities
  • Develop internal AI champions
Phase 3 — Transformation
Transform
Months 18–36
  • Deploy advanced AI (predictive, agentic)
  • AI embedded in decision-making processes
  • Cross-department AI collaboration
  • Continuous optimization and innovation
  • Thought leadership and peer benchmarking

Planning Principle

Quick wins aren't just about easy projects — they're about proving value to skeptics and building organizational trust in AI. Choose Phase 1 initiatives that are visible, measurable, and meaningful to frontline staff.

Appendix — Compliance Quick-Check
Appendix

Compliance Quick-Check

If you're a government agency or receive federal funding, use this checklist to assess your alignment with key AI governance requirements.

OMB M-25-21 Readiness Checklist

Check each item you've completed or have in progress. Items left unchecked represent compliance gaps to address in your roadmap.

  • Completed an inventory of all AI use cases across the organization
  • Classified each use case as safety-impacting, rights-impacting, or neither
  • Designated a Chief AI Officer or equivalent governance lead
  • Established an AI governance board or review committee
  • Created risk assessment processes aligned with NIST AI RMF
  • Developed impact assessments for rights-impacting AI systems
  • Implemented human oversight mechanisms for high-risk AI systems
  • Created transparency documentation for public-facing AI
  • Established AI incident reporting and response procedures
  • Built ongoing monitoring and evaluation processes
  • Prepared required agency AI governance plan documentation
  • Set up reporting templates for ongoing compliance obligations
