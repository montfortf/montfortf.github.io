The Challenge

Agencies Rush to AI Without Understanding Their Starting Point

Executive mandates and public expectations are pushing agencies to adopt AI quickly. But launching AI initiatives without understanding your current capabilities leads to failed pilots, wasted budgets, and eroded trust.

A rigorous readiness assessment--covering data quality, infrastructure, workforce skills, governance maturity, and organizational culture--gives leadership the clarity to invest wisely and build on a solid foundation. According to Gartner, organizations that conduct formal AI readiness assessments are 2.5x more likely to achieve production-scale AI within 18 months.

Without an AI Readiness Assessment:

  • ✕ AI pilots fail because data isn't clean, accessible, or governed
  • ✕ Investments target wrong use cases for your maturity level
  • ✕ Staff resistance stalls adoption due to skills and culture gaps
  • ✕ Compliance risks from missing governance and ethics frameworks
Our Methodology

Four-Dimension Assessment Framework

Aligned with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework and proven across federal, state, and local government agencies.

Phase 1

Discovery and Scoping

We begin with executive stakeholder interviews and document review to understand your mission priorities, current technology landscape, and strategic objectives for AI. This phase calibrates the assessment to your specific context.

  • Executive and technical stakeholder interviews
  • Existing technology and data inventory review
  • Mission priority and use case alignment
  • Assessment scope and dimension weighting
Phase 2

Multi-Dimension Evaluation

Deep assessment across all four readiness dimensions using structured rubrics, technical audits, and workforce surveys. Each dimension is scored on a 1-5 maturity scale with specific, observable criteria.

  • Data quality, accessibility, and governance audit
  • Infrastructure and platform capability evaluation
  • Organizational culture and change capacity survey
  • Policy, ethics, and compliance framework review
Phase 3

Gap Analysis and Benchmarking

We map your current state against your AI ambitions and benchmark against peer agencies to identify the most critical gaps. Each gap is prioritized by impact and effort to address.

  • Current vs. target state gap identification
  • Peer benchmarking against similar agencies
  • Risk and compliance gap mapping (NIST AI RMF, FedRAMP)
  • Priority ranking by impact and remediation effort
Phase 4

Roadmap and Executive Briefing

We deliver a prioritized readiness roadmap with quick wins, medium-term capability building, and long-term transformation milestones. An executive briefing ensures leadership alignment and commitment.

  • Phased readiness improvement roadmap
  • Quick-win initiative identification
  • Investment and resource planning guidance
  • Executive briefing and leadership alignment session

Why Assessment Matters

You Can't Build AI on a Foundation You Don't Understand

AI readiness isn't a checkbox--it's a strategic input that shapes every decision that follows. A thorough assessment ensures you're:

  • Investing where it matters most--targeting gaps that actually block AI progress, not just visible ones
  • Choosing the right starting point--selecting use cases matched to your actual maturity level
  • Meeting compliance requirements--ensuring alignment with NIST AI RMF, OMB mandates, and agency-specific policies
  • Building stakeholder confidence--giving leadership and oversight bodies evidence-based assurance
