The Problem

LLMs Don't Know Your Data

Out-of-the-box language models are trained on general knowledge, not your policies, procedures, and program rules. They can hallucinate, provide outdated information, or simply guess when asked about your specific context.

Without RAG

  • Generic responses not specific to your agency
  • Risk of hallucinated information
  • No awareness of policy changes
  • Cannot cite authoritative sources

With RAG

  • Answers grounded in your documents
  • Citations to authoritative sources
  • Always current with your latest content
  • Reduced hallucination risk
How It Works

RAG Architecture

1

Index

Your documents are processed, chunked, and stored in a vector database with semantic embeddings.

2

Retrieve

When a question is asked, relevant document chunks are retrieved based on semantic similarity.

3

Augment

Retrieved context is added to the prompt, grounding the LLM in your authoritative content.

4

Generate

The LLM generates a response based on the retrieved context, with citations to sources.
