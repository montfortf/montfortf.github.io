The Challenge

The AI Platform Landscape Is Overwhelming--and the Stakes Are High

Government agencies face a rapidly expanding marketplace of AI platforms, each with different compliance postures, pricing models, and capability profiles. Choosing wrong means years of lock-in, costly migrations, and mission-critical systems built on the wrong foundation.

Vendor sales teams present best-case scenarios. Your agency needs an independent advisor who evaluates platforms against your specific requirements--including FedRAMP authorization, data sovereignty, integration with legacy systems, and realistic total cost of ownership over 3-5 years.

Common Platform Selection Mistakes:

  • ✕ Selecting based on demos, not production requirements
  • ✕ Ignoring FedRAMP/StateRAMP authorization status
  • ✕ Underestimating integration costs with existing systems
  • ✕ Vendor lock-in from proprietary architectures
Our Framework

Structured Evaluation Process

A rigorous, vendor-neutral methodology that delivers defensible recommendations backed by evidence and aligned with procurement requirements.

Phase 1

Requirements Definition

We work with your technical and program teams to define comprehensive evaluation criteria weighted to your priorities. This ensures the evaluation measures what matters most to your agency.

  • Functional and non-functional requirements gathering
  • Compliance and security requirements mapping
  • Integration requirements with legacy systems
  • Weighted scoring criteria development
Phase 2

Market Scan and Shortlisting

We survey the full landscape of relevant AI platforms, filter by compliance readiness and baseline fit, and produce a shortlist of 3-5 platforms for deep evaluation.

  • Comprehensive market landscape analysis
  • FedRAMP/StateRAMP/HIPAA compliance verification
  • Initial capability-to-requirement mapping
  • Shortlist recommendation with rationale
Phase 3

Deep-Dive Evaluation

Each shortlisted platform undergoes hands-on evaluation including proof-of-concept testing, architecture review, reference checks with government peers, and detailed cost modeling.

  • Proof-of-concept with your data and use cases
  • Architecture and security review
  • Government reference customer interviews
  • 5-year total cost of ownership modeling
Phase 4

Recommendation and Procurement Support

We deliver a scored comparison with our recommendation and rationale. We can also support your procurement process with RFP development, vendor demonstrations, and contract negotiation guidance.

  • Scored platform comparison matrix
  • Risk assessment for each platform
  • Implementation planning for recommended platform
  • Optional RFP and procurement support
What We Evaluate

Six Evaluation Dimensions

Every platform is scored against six critical dimensions that determine long-term success in government environments.

Functional Fit

How well the platform meets your specific use case requirements--from NLP and computer vision to predictive analytics and agentic AI capabilities. We test with your actual data and workflows.

Government Compliance

Verified FedRAMP, StateRAMP, HIPAA, CJIS, and other compliance certifications. We validate authorization status, boundary definitions, and continuous monitoring posture--not just marketing claims.

Integration Capability

Ability to connect with your existing systems--legacy databases, ERP platforms, case management systems, and data warehouses. We assess API maturity, ETL capabilities, and middleware compatibility.

Total Cost of Ownership

Full 5-year cost modeling including licensing, implementation, integration, training, operations, and scaling. We surface hidden costs that vendor proposals often obscure.

Vendor Viability

Financial stability, government market commitment, support model for public sector clients, and product roadmap alignment. We verify government references and contract vehicle availability.

Scalability and Performance

Ability to grow with your needs--from pilot to enterprise scale. We evaluate performance under load, multi-agency support, and architectural capacity for future AI model advancements.
