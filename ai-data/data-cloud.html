The Challenge

Your Data Lives in Silos

Constituents don't experience life in program silos. But your data does. Information about the same family lives in multiple systems that don't talk to each other -- making coordinated service delivery nearly impossible and putting OPEN Data Act compliance at risk.

Without a unified data platform, agencies struggle to meet CDO Council mandates for data sharing, face audit risks from fragmented records, and cannot leverage AI for mission-critical decisions. Data sovereignty requirements add further complexity when constituent PII spans jurisdictional boundaries.

Without Unified Data:

  • Staff can't see the full picture of constituent needs
  • Constituents repeat their story at every touchpoint
  • Programs can't coordinate services effectively
  • AI and analytics lack the data foundation to deliver value
  • OPEN Data Act and CDO Council compliance gaps grow
Our Approach

Four-Phase Data Cloud Implementation

A structured, compliance-first approach that delivers unified constituent data while maintaining FedRAMP authorization and data sovereignty controls.

Phase 1

Discovery and Data Mapping

Inventory all constituent data sources, map data flows, and identify integration requirements. Assess current data sovereignty posture and compliance obligations.

  • Comprehensive data source inventory
  • Data flow mapping and dependency analysis
  • FedRAMP and IRS 1075 compliance assessment
  • Data sovereignty and jurisdictional review
Phase 2

Data Model and Governance Design

Design the unified data model, identity resolution rules, and governance framework aligned with CDO Council best practices and OPEN Data Act requirements.

  • Unified constituent data model design
  • Identity resolution and matching rules
  • Data governance framework and stewardship roles
  • OPEN Data Act alignment and metadata standards
Phase 3

Integration and Unification

Connect data sources, implement identity resolution, and build the 360-degree constituent view within the FedRAMP-authorized environment.

  • Source system connector configuration
  • Real-time and batch data ingestion pipelines
  • Identity resolution and profile unification
  • Data quality monitoring and alerting
Phase 4

Activation and AI Enablement

Activate unified data across channels, enable AI capabilities, and establish ongoing governance for sustained value and compliance.

  • Cross-channel data activation
  • Einstein AI and Agentforce enablement
  • Segmentation and journey orchestration
  • Ongoing governance and compliance monitoring

Why Data Cloud Matters

The Foundation for AI-Powered Government

Data Cloud isn't just about integration -- it's the prerequisite for every AI initiative. Without unified, high-quality data, AI delivers noise instead of insight.

  • OPEN Data Act aligned -- metadata standards and data inventories that satisfy federal mandates
  • CDO Council compliant -- governance framework that maps to CDO Council data sharing principles
  • Data sovereignty first -- constituent PII stays within jurisdictional boundaries with full audit trails
  • AI-ready foundation -- clean, unified data that powers Einstein, Agentforce, and custom ML models
