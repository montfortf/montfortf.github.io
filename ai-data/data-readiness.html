The Reality

Data Challenges Block AI Success

Most AI projects don't fail because of AI technology -- they fail because of data. Government agencies have rich data, but it's often siloed, inconsistent, incomplete, or poorly documented. Gartner predicts that through 2026, organizations will abandon 60% of AI projects unsupported by AI-ready data.

The CDO Council has made data readiness a top priority, and the OPEN Data Act requires agencies to maintain comprehensive data inventories with machine-readable metadata. Without addressing these foundations, AI initiatives stall before they start -- and data sovereignty risks multiply as agencies share data across jurisdictional boundaries.

Common Data Challenges:

  • Data trapped in silos across legacy systems
  • Inconsistent formats, definitions, and standards
  • Missing values, duplicates, and quality gaps
  • No data dictionary, catalog, or lineage documentation
  • Unclear ownership, governance, and stewardship
Our Approach

Four-Phase Data Readiness Process

A structured methodology that transforms raw, fragmented government data into AI-ready assets -- with governance and compliance built in from the start.

Phase 1

Discover

Inventory all data sources, understand the current state, map to AI use cases, and assess compliance posture against OPEN Data Act requirements and CDO Council standards.

  • Comprehensive data source inventory
  • AI use case mapping and data requirements
  • OPEN Data Act compliance gap analysis
  • Data sovereignty and jurisdictional assessment
Phase 2

Assess

Profile data quality across every dimension, evaluate gaps against AI requirements, and produce a scored readiness assessment with prioritized remediation recommendations.

  • Data quality profiling (completeness, accuracy, consistency, timeliness)
  • AI readiness scoring and benchmarking
  • Governance maturity evaluation
  • Prioritized remediation roadmap
Phase 3

Remediate

Cleanse, integrate, and transform data to meet AI requirements. Build ETL/ELT pipelines, implement master data management, and create feature stores for machine learning.

  • Data cleansing, standardization, and deduplication
  • Integration pipelines and entity resolution
  • Feature engineering and training dataset creation
  • Metadata enrichment and catalog development
Phase 4

Sustain

Establish governance frameworks, data stewardship roles, automated quality monitoring, and ongoing compliance processes that keep data AI-ready over time.

  • Data governance framework and stewardship program
  • Automated data quality monitoring and alerting
  • CDO Council aligned reporting and metrics
  • Continuous compliance and audit readiness

Why Data Readiness Matters

The Difference Between AI Success and Failure

Data readiness isn't a prerequisite you check off -- it's the ongoing discipline that determines whether your AI investments produce trusted results or expensive noise.

  • CDO Council aligned -- governance frameworks that map to federal data sharing and stewardship standards
  • OPEN Data Act compliant -- machine-readable metadata, data inventories, and documentation that satisfy federal mandates
  • Data sovereignty preserved -- constituent PII protected with jurisdictional controls, audit trails, and access governance
  • AI lifecycle ready -- data quality assessed during concept phase, provisioned during development, maintained through deployment
