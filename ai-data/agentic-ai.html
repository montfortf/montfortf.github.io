Beyond Chatbots

What is Agentic AI?

Agentic AI goes beyond simple chatbots or single-task automation. AI agents can plan, reason, use tools, and complete multi-step processes—like a smart assistant that actually gets things done.

Unlike traditional automation that follows rigid rules, AI agents understand context, make decisions, and adapt to changing situations. They can research information, draft documents, coordinate workflows, and complete complex tasks autonomously—while keeping humans informed and in control of consequential decisions.

Built on Salesforce Agentforce 2.0, our agents leverage multi-agent orchestration and agent mesh architecture to coordinate across systems. Multiple specialized agents collaborate in real time—sharing context, delegating tasks, and escalating to humans when needed—delivering outcomes no single agent could achieve alone.

Responsible Agentic AI

Humans Stay in Control

In government, the stakes are too high for AI to make consequential decisions autonomously. That's why every agent we build keeps humans informed and in control—aligned with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework and OMB M-24-10 requirements for responsible AI use.

Our approach meets EO 14110 AI Executive Order compliance standards, ensuring every deployment prioritizes safety, security, and trustworthiness across the AI lifecycle.

AI agents handle research, preparation, and routine tasks—but humans approve all consequential decisions. The agent presents recommendations with evidence; staff make the final call.

Every action the agent takes is logged and visible. Staff can see exactly what the agent did, why it did it, and what information it used. No black boxes.

Staff can override agent recommendations at any point. Corrections feed back into the system, improving future performance. The agent learns from human judgment.

Complete logging of all agent interactions for compliance, quality assurance, and continuous improvement. Every decision can be traced back to its inputs.

Agents operate within defined boundaries. They know what they can and can't do, and they escalate appropriately when situations fall outside their scope.
