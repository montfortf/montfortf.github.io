The Challenge

Constituents Need Help Now -- Not During Business Hours

Constituents have questions at all hours. They need help navigating complex programs, understanding eligibility, and completing applications. But contact centers operate limited hours, hold times stretch past 30 minutes, and staff answer the same questions hundreds of times per day.

Meanwhile, nearly 90 percent of federal agencies now use some form of AI, and 60 percent of governments are prioritizing process automation in 2026. Conversational AI is the frontline of this transformation -- delivering immediate constituent value while freeing human agents for complex cases that require empathy and judgment.

The Constituent Experience Gap:

  • Limited contact center hours exclude working families
  • 30+ minute hold times frustrate and deter constituents
  • Staff answer same routine questions hundreds of times daily
  • Language barriers prevent equitable access to services
Our Approach

Conversational AI Implementation

A human-centered methodology that prioritizes constituent experience, accessibility, and responsible AI principles from day one.

Phase 1

Discovery & Conversation Design

Analyze constituent needs, map high-volume inquiry patterns, and design conversation flows that feel natural and resolve issues efficiently. We review call center transcripts, FAQ analytics, and constituent feedback to identify the highest-impact automation opportunities.

  • Call center data analysis and inquiry pattern mapping
  • Constituent journey mapping and pain point identification
  • Conversation flow design with empathy principles
  • Accessibility and multilingual requirements assessment
  • Integration requirements for backend systems
Phase 2

Knowledge Base & AI Configuration

Build the knowledge foundation using RAG architecture to ground every response in your authoritative content. Configure the LLM for your agency's tone, compliance requirements, and escalation policies.

  • RAG knowledge base from policies, FAQs, and procedures
  • Intent classification and entity extraction setup
  • Guardrails for sensitive topics and PII handling
  • Escalation logic and human handoff configuration
  • Multilingual support and plain-language optimization
Phase 3

Integration & Channel Deployment

Connect the conversational AI to your backend systems for personalized service and deploy across all constituent touchpoints -- web, mobile, SMS, voice IVR, and agent-assist tools.

  • CRM, case management, and eligibility system integration
  • Secure authentication for personalized queries
  • Omnichannel deployment (web, mobile, SMS, voice)
  • Agent-assist mode for contact center staff
  • Section 508 accessibility compliance
Phase 4

Testing, Launch & Continuous Improvement

Rigorous testing with real constituent scenarios, phased rollout with monitoring, and continuous optimization based on conversation analytics and user feedback.

  • User acceptance testing with diverse constituent groups
  • Phased rollout with A/B testing
  • Conversation analytics and satisfaction monitoring
  • Continuous content and flow optimization
  • Regular bias and accuracy auditing

The Impact

Better Service at Lower Cost

Deloitte research found that automating federal employee tasks could save 96.7 million to 1.2 billion work hours annually. Conversational AI delivers this efficiency while improving -- not diminishing -- the constituent experience.

  • Instant answers -- no hold times, no phone trees, no business-hours restrictions
  • Consistent accuracy -- every constituent gets the same correct information
  • Staff empowerment -- agents freed to handle complex cases requiring human judgment
  • Equitable access -- multilingual, 24/7, accessible across all channels
