The Challenge

Government Runs on Reports, Not Insights

Most government agencies generate mountains of reports but still lack the real-time visibility needed for effective decision-making. Static spreadsheets and quarterly PDF reports can't keep pace with the speed of constituent needs or legislative demands.

The OPEN Data Act requires agencies to make data accessible and useful, but without modern analytics capabilities, compliance becomes a box-checking exercise rather than a catalyst for better outcomes. CDO Council guidance emphasizes data-driven decision-making, yet many agencies still rely on intuition over evidence when allocating resources and measuring program impact.

Common Analytics Gaps:

  • Reports are static and weeks behind reality
  • Data analysts are bottlenecks for every question
  • No predictive capabilities to anticipate demand
  • Federal reporting requires manual data wrangling
  • Program outcomes are measured retroactively, not proactively
Our Approach

Analytics Maturity Journey

We meet agencies where they are and build capabilities progressively -- from foundational reporting to AI-powered prescriptive analytics.

Stage 1

Descriptive Analytics

Build the foundation with modern dashboards and automated reporting that answer "what happened?" in real time, replacing static spreadsheets and manual processes.

  • Executive dashboards and operational scorecards
  • Automated federal and state reporting
  • KPI tracking and performance monitoring
  • Data warehouse design and implementation
Stage 2

Diagnostic Analytics

Add drill-down and root-cause analysis capabilities that answer "why did it happen?" -- enabling analysts and program managers to explore data and uncover patterns.

  • Interactive drill-down and filtering
  • Root cause analysis frameworks
  • Self-service analytics for business users
  • Ad-hoc analysis and data exploration tools
Stage 3

Predictive Analytics

Layer in forecasting and trend prediction that answer "what will happen?" -- allowing agencies to anticipate demand, identify at-risk populations, and allocate resources proactively.

  • Demand forecasting and trend prediction
  • Risk scoring and early warning systems
  • Statistical modeling and ML integration
  • Geospatial and temporal analysis
Stage 4

Prescriptive Analytics

Deploy AI-powered recommendations that answer "what should we do?" -- delivering optimized resource allocation, next-best-action guidance, and automated decision support.

  • AI-powered recommendations and next-best-action
  • Resource optimization and scenario modeling
  • Automated decision support systems
  • Natural language query and AI-generated insights

Why Analytics Matters

From Reporting Compliance to Mission Intelligence

Modern analytics transforms government from reactive to proactive -- moving beyond compliance reporting to real-time mission intelligence that drives better outcomes for constituents.

  • OPEN Data Act transparency -- public-facing dashboards and open data portals that satisfy federal transparency mandates
  • CDO Council data literacy -- self-service tools and training programs that build organizational analytics capability
  • Data sovereignty built in -- governed access controls, audit trails, and PII protections embedded in every dashboard and report
  • Evidence-based policy -- analytics that connect program spending to constituent outcomes for legislative accountability
