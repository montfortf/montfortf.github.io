The Challenge

The Government AI Skills Gap Is Widening

Federal agencies are mandated to adopt AI, yet fewer than 1 in 5 government employees report having the skills to work effectively with AI tools. The U.S. Office of Personnel Management has identified AI workforce readiness as a top-tier priority, calling for structured training programs at every level of government.

Without deliberate investment in AI education, agencies face mounting risks: failed deployments driven by untrained users, ethical blind spots from teams unfamiliar with responsible AI principles, and a growing inability to attract and retain technical talent against private-sector competition.

Without AI Training:

  • Staff resist or misuse AI tools
  • Leaders cannot evaluate AI proposals
  • Bias and compliance risks go undetected
  • Agency loses talent to the private sector
Our Approach

Role-Based Learning Tracks

Every role in government interacts with AI differently. Our tiered curriculum ensures each audience gets the depth and context they need -- from strategic oversight to hands-on prompt engineering.

Track 1

Executive AI Leadership

Half-day immersion for agency heads, CIOs, deputy directors, and program executives. Designed to equip decision-makers with the strategic vocabulary and evaluative framework to sponsor and govern AI initiatives responsibly.

  • AI fundamentals, terminology, and capability landscape
  • Strategic opportunity assessment and risk evaluation
  • Responsible AI leadership and governance obligations
  • Building an AI-ready organization and culture
  • Budget justification and ROI frameworks for AI
Track 2

AI for Government Managers

Full-day workshop for program managers, supervisors, and team leads who must translate AI strategy into operational reality. Covers practical skills for identifying use cases, managing AI vendor relationships, and leading teams through technology transitions.

  • How AI and machine learning actually work (demystified)
  • Government-specific AI use cases and success stories
  • Identifying and prioritizing AI opportunities in your division
  • Managing AI projects: scope, timelines, and vendor oversight
  • Change leadership during AI-driven process redesign
Track 3

AI Practitioner Fundamentals

Two-day intensive for IT staff, data analysts, and technical power users who will build, configure, or maintain AI systems. Participants gain hands-on experience with real tools and government-relevant datasets.

  • Core AI and machine learning concepts and model types
  • Data requirements, quality standards, and pipeline design
  • Working with large language models and AI platforms
  • Prompt engineering, RAG architectures, and fine-tuning basics
  • AI testing, validation, and performance monitoring
Track 4

Responsible AI Certification

One-day certification program for AI project teams, compliance officers, and anyone involved in AI governance. Participants complete hands-on exercises and earn a Brite.AI Responsible AI Practitioner certification upon passing the assessment.

  • Ethical AI principles and government-specific obligations
  • Algorithmic bias detection, measurement, and mitigation
  • Transparency, explainability, and public trust requirements
  • AI compliance frameworks (EO 14110, NIST AI RMF, OMB M-24-10)
  • Audit readiness and continuous monitoring protocols

Why Invest in AI Training

Training Is the Single Biggest Predictor of AI Success

According to Deloitte's 2026 State of AI in the Enterprise report, insufficient worker skills are the single largest barrier to integrating AI into workflows.   • Meets federal mandates -- aligns to AI Executive Order training requirements and OMB M-24-10 guidance
