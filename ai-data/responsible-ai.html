The Challenge

AI Without Guardrails Puts Agencies at Risk

Government AI systems make decisions that affect people's lives--benefits eligibility, child welfare interventions, healthcare access, criminal justice outcomes. Without a responsible AI framework, agencies face regulatory penalties under EO 14110, civil rights violations, erosion of public trust, and systems that disproportionately harm vulnerable populations.

The OMB Memorandum M-24-10 now requires federal agencies to implement AI governance frameworks, conduct impact assessments, and maintain public AI use inventories. Agencies without a structured responsible AI program risk non-compliance, GAO audit findings, and congressional scrutiny. A proactive framework transforms regulatory burden into a competitive advantage for mission delivery.

Without a Responsible AI Framework:

  • ✕ Non-compliance with EO 14110 and OMB M-24-10 mandates
  • ✕ Algorithmic bias causing disparate impact on protected groups
  • ✕ GAO audit findings and congressional scrutiny
  • ✕ Loss of public trust from opaque decision-making
Our Methodology

Five-Phase Framework Development

A structured approach grounded in NIST AI RMF functions--Govern, Map, Measure, Manage--that delivers an actionable, audit-ready responsible AI program.

Phase 1

Maturity Assessment

Evaluate your current AI governance posture against NIST AI RMF, EO 14110 requirements, the AI Bill of Rights principles, and agency-specific mandates.

  • Responsible AI maturity model evaluation
  • Existing policy and process inventory
  • Stakeholder interview program (executive, technical, legal)
  • Gap analysis against NIST AI RMF and OMB M-24-10
Phase 2

Principles and Governance Design

Establish organization-wide AI ethics principles, governance structures, and accountability mechanisms aligned with your mission and values.

  • Core ethical principles statement co-developed with leadership
  • AI governance board charter and composition
  • Role definitions (AI Ethics Officer, Data Stewards, Review Board)
  • Decision-authority matrix for AI deployment approvals
Phase 3

Risk and Impact Framework

Build structured risk categorization and algorithmic impact assessment processes aligned with NIST AI RMF functions.

  • AI risk tiering methodology (Low / Medium / High / Unacceptable)
  • Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA) templates and workflows
  • NIST AI RMF GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE implementation
  • Civil rights and equity impact evaluation criteria
Phase 4

Policy and Controls Implementation

Develop comprehensive policies, technical controls, and operational procedures that operationalize responsible AI principles.

  • AI ethics policy suite (procurement, development, deployment)
  • Bias testing and fairness standards (linked to Bias Audit service)
  • Explainability requirements by use-case risk level
  • Human oversight protocols and override procedures
Phase 5

Operationalization and Culture

Embed responsible AI into daily operations, procurement processes, and organizational culture for long-term sustainability.

  • Training and certification program for staff and leadership
  • AI incident response and remediation playbook
  • Continuous monitoring dashboards and reporting cadence
  • Vendor and procurement responsible AI requirements
