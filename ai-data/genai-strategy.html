The Opportunity

Generative AI Can Transform Government--But Only With the Right Approach

From automating document creation to enabling natural language access to complex policy information, generative AI offers transformative potential for government agencies. McKinsey estimates gen AI could unlock $2.6-$4.4 trillion in annual value across industries.

But GenAI also introduces new challenges around accuracy, security, bias, and appropriate use in public-trust environments. The agencies succeeding with GenAI are those that explore opportunities in a controlled, structured way--with NIST AI RMF-aligned guardrails, human oversight, and clear governance from day one.

High-Value GenAI Use Cases for Government

  • Document drafting, summarization, and review
  • Constituent Q&A and intelligent self-service
  • Policy and procedure lookup with natural language
  • Automated report generation and data analysis
  • Training content and knowledge base development
Our Approach

Structured Path to GenAI Value

A phased approach that balances exploration with appropriate risk management--moving from education to production with confidence.

Phase 1

Education and Alignment

Build leadership understanding of GenAI capabilities, limitations, and governance requirements. This phase creates shared vocabulary and realistic expectations across the organization.

  • Executive GenAI education workshops
  • Capability and limitation briefings
  • Government-specific risk and compliance overview
  • Vision alignment and success criteria definition
Phase 2

Use Case Identification and Prioritization

Systematically evaluate GenAI opportunities based on value, risk, feasibility, and alignment with NIST AI RMF guidelines. We map each use case to required guardrails and governance levels.

  • Cross-functional use case discovery workshops
  • Value-risk-feasibility scoring framework
  • Guardrail and governance level mapping
  • Pilot candidate selection and scoping
Phase 3

Controlled Pilot with Guardrails

Deploy GenAI for the selected use case in a controlled environment with real users, comprehensive monitoring, and human-in-the-loop review workflows. We measure accuracy, adoption, and impact.

  • Secure, FedRAMP-aligned deployment environment
  • Content filtering and hallucination prevention
  • Human review workflows for all outputs
  • User feedback collection and iteration
Phase 4

Evaluate, Refine, and Scale

Assess pilot results against success criteria, refine the approach based on learnings, and develop a scaling roadmap for broader GenAI deployment across the organization.

  • Quantitative and qualitative impact assessment
  • Governance and policy framework refinement
  • Enterprise scaling roadmap development
  • Ongoing monitoring and improvement plan

Why Structured GenAI Adoption Matters

The Agencies Succeeding With GenAI Aren't Experimenting Randomly

Generative AI in government demands a different approach than the private sector. Every deployment must account for:

  • Public trust and accuracy--citizens deserve correct information, not AI hallucinations
  • Equity and bias prevention--AI outputs must be fair across all populations served
  • Data sovereignty and security--sensitive government data requires FedRAMP-level protection
  • Regulatory compliance--OMB mandates, NIST AI RMF, and agency-specific policies must be met
