The Challenge

AI Innovation Cannot Come at the Cost of Security

Government agencies want to leverage generative AI but face legitimate security concerns. Public AI services may train on your data, lack FedRAMP authorization, and create unacceptable risks around sensitive information. The FedRAMP 20x program is accelerating AI cloud authorizations, but agencies still need purpose-built secure platforms.

With the 2026 Vulnerability Detection and Response standard replacing previous monitoring requirements, agencies must implement platforms that address rapid-turnaround AI threats while enabling fast remediation -- all within their authorized security boundary.

Security Risks of Public AI:

  • ✕ Data may be used for third-party model training
  • ✕ Lack of FedRAMP or StateRAMP authorization
  • ✕ No visibility into data handling or retention
  • ✕ Potential for sensitive data leakage in responses
  • ✕ Cannot meet IL4/IL5 data classification requirements
Platform Capabilities

Security-First AI Architecture

Every layer of the platform is built for government security requirements -- from infrastructure to application, with NIST 800-53 controls embedded at every level.

Private Deployment

Deploy AI models in your own government cloud environment or on-premise data center. Data never leaves your security boundary. Supports AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, and air-gapped deployments for IL4/IL5 workloads.

End-to-End Encryption

FIPS 140-2 validated encryption for data at rest and in transit. Hardware security modules (HSMs) for key management. Customer-managed encryption keys ensure complete data sovereignty.

Zero-Trust Access Control

Role-based access control with attribute-based policies, SSO integration via SAML 2.0/OIDC, multi-factor authentication, and continuous session validation. Every access request is verified, never trusted.

Complete Audit Logging

Immutable audit trails of all AI interactions, queries, responses, and administrative actions. Tamper-proof logs stored within your boundary support NIST 800-53 AU controls and ATO evidence requirements.

Data Loss Prevention

Automated content filtering prevents sensitive data -- PII, PHI, classified information -- from being included in prompts or leaked in AI responses. Custom DLP policies aligned to your data classification scheme.

Security Monitoring & SIEM

Real-time security event monitoring, anomaly detection, and SIEM integration. Automated threat response aligned with the 2026 Vulnerability Detection and Response standard for rapid AI-specific threat remediation.
