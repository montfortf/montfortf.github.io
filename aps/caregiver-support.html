The Challenge

Caregivers Are Burning Out Without Coordinated Support

Over 53 million Americans serve as unpaid family caregivers, and the toll is staggering. More than one in five report declining health, financial strain, and emotional exhaustion. Yet most aging services programs have no coordinated way to reach, support, or track these caregivers.

Respite care scheduling happens through phone tag. Support groups are discovered by word of mouth. Burnout goes undetected until a crisis forces placement. OAA Title III-E mandates caregiver support services, but without a digital platform, programs struggle to deliver — and demonstrate — that support effectively.

Without a Caregiver Platform:

  • ✕ Caregivers isolated without peer support
  • ✕ Respite care scheduling done manually by phone
  • ✕ No burnout prevention screening in place
  • ✕ OAA Title III-E tracking & reporting gaps
Our Approach

Four-Phase Platform Development

A caregiver-centered approach that builds engagement before scaling features.

Phase 1

Caregiver Needs Assessment

Survey and interview active caregivers to understand their daily challenges, digital literacy, support preferences, and unmet needs.

  • Caregiver survey & interview program
  • Service gap analysis across care types
  • Digital readiness & accessibility review
  • OAA Title III-E compliance requirements mapping
Phase 2

Platform Design

Design an intuitive, mobile-first platform with caregiver personas guiding every interaction, from first visit to ongoing engagement.

  • Caregiver persona & journey mapping
  • Mobile-first interface design
  • Respite scheduling workflow architecture
  • Support group matching algorithm design
Phase 3

Feature Development

Build core features iteratively, starting with respite scheduling and expanding to support groups, training, and wellness assessments.

  • Respite care booking & management system
  • Support group matching & virtual meeting tools
  • Training resource library with progress tracking
  • Burnout assessment & wellness monitoring
Phase 4

Outreach & Adoption

Launch targeted outreach to reach caregivers where they are — doctors’ offices, community centers, faith organizations — and build sustained engagement.

  • Multi-channel caregiver outreach campaign
  • Healthcare provider referral partnerships
  • Community organization onboarding
  • Engagement monitoring & retention optimization

Why It Matters

Supporting Caregivers Prevents Crises for Everyone

When caregivers are supported, the entire care ecosystem benefits — fewer emergency placements, better outcomes for care recipients, and stronger communities:

  • ✓ Support caregivers before they reach crisis — proactive screening catches burnout early
  • ✓ Connect to respite & support services — remove scheduling barriers that prevent utilization
  • ✓ Track caregiver wellness proactively — longitudinal data identifies trends before crises occur
  • ✓ Meet OAA Title III-E requirements — automated tracking and reporting ensures federal compliance
