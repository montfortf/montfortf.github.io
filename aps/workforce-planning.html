The Challenge

APS Programs Are Understaffed & Under Pressure

The national APS investigator shortage is worsening. High turnover rates, rising caseloads, and an aging population mean demand is outpacing supply. Yet most agencies lack data-driven staffing models to justify new positions or optimize existing resources.

The Administration for Community Living’s (ACL) Workload Toolkit provides a framework for analyzing APS staffing needs, but most programs underutilize it—missing the opportunity to make evidence-based budget requests and reduce investigator burnout.

Without Workforce Planning:

  • ✕ Understaffed programs with no data to justify positions
  • ✕ Burnout drives experienced investigators out
  • ✕ Caseloads assigned without workload analysis
  • ✕ Budget requests lack supporting data
Our Approach

Four-Phase Workforce Engagement

A data-driven methodology that turns workforce challenges into actionable staffing strategies.

Phase 1

Current State Workforce Analysis

Comprehensive assessment of your existing workforce including staffing levels, turnover patterns, caseload volumes, and workload distribution across all roles and regions.

  • Staffing level inventory and trend analysis
  • Turnover rate analysis by role and region
  • Caseload volume and complexity mapping
  • ACL Workload Toolkit baseline assessment
Phase 2

Staffing Model Development

Build predictive staffing models using the ACL Workload Toolkit framework, calibrated with your actual caseload data and investigation complexity patterns.

  • ACL Workload Toolkit full implementation
  • Case complexity weighting models
  • Demand forecasting by abuse type
  • Staffing gap quantification
Phase 3

Implementation Planning

Translate staffing models into actionable plans including budget justifications, phased hiring recommendations, and caseload redistribution strategies.

  • Budget justification documentation
  • Phased hiring roadmap
  • Caseload redistribution plan
  • Retention strategy development
Phase 4

Continuous Optimization

Establish ongoing workforce monitoring with automated dashboards, early-warning indicators for burnout, and quarterly model recalibration.

  • Automated workforce dashboards
  • Burnout early-warning indicators
  • Quarterly model recalibration
  • Legislative session budget support
