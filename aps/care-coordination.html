The Challenge

Siloed Services Leave Vulnerable Adults Behind

Older adults and vulnerable populations interact with multiple agencies — APS, Area Agencies on Aging, Medicaid waiver programs, behavioral health providers, and community organizations — yet none of these systems talk to each other.

Clients are forced to navigate a fragmented landscape, repeating their stories at every intake. Caseworkers lack a unified view of a person’s needs, leading to service gaps, duplicated efforts, and missed opportunities for early intervention.

Without Care Coordination:

  • ✕ Duplicated assessments across agencies
  • ✕ Clients falling through service gaps
  • ✕ No unified person view across programs
  • ✕ Fragmented care plans with conflicting goals
Our Approach

Four-Phase Coordination Engagement

A structured path from siloed operations to unified, person-centered coordination.

Phase 1

Stakeholder Mapping

Identify every agency, provider, and community organization that touches your population. Map data flows, referral patterns, and service overlaps.

  • Cross-agency stakeholder interviews
  • Service delivery workflow documentation
  • Data sharing agreement inventory
  • Referral pattern analysis
Phase 2

Unified Data Architecture

Design the shared data model that connects disparate systems while respecting privacy, consent, and data governance requirements.

  • Master person index design
  • Consent management framework
  • Data integration specifications
  • Privacy & security compliance review
Phase 3

Platform Configuration

Build the unified coordination platform with shared intake, cross-agency referrals, and collaborative service planning tools.

  • Unified client profile implementation
  • Cross-agency referral workflows
  • Collaborative service plan builder
  • Outcomes tracking configuration
Phase 4

Agency Onboarding

Bring partner agencies online with phased onboarding, training, and ongoing support to ensure adoption across the network.

  • Agency-specific training programs
  • Phased go-live with pilot cohorts
  • Change management & adoption support
  • Continuous improvement feedback loops
