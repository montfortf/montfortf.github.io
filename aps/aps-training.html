The Challenge

APS Workforce Training Is Inconsistent & Outdated

High turnover rates across APS programs mean agencies are constantly onboarding new investigators who lack standardized training. Meanwhile, new AI-powered tools require entirely new competencies that existing curricula do not address.

NAPSA’s National Training Center curriculum provides a strong foundation, but most agencies struggle to implement it consistently while also integrating modern technology training into their programs.

Without Structured Training:

  • ✕ Inconsistent investigation quality across staff
  • ✕ New staff unprepared for complex cases
  • ✕ AI tools adopted slowly or used incorrectly
  • ✕ NAPSA training standards not met
Our Approach

Four-Phase Training Engagement

A structured methodology that builds lasting workforce competency, not just one-time workshops.

Phase 1

Training Needs Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of current skill gaps, role requirements, and NAPSA curriculum alignment across your APS workforce.

  • Role-based competency mapping
  • Current training program audit
  • NAPSA curriculum gap analysis
  • AI tool readiness evaluation
Phase 2

Curriculum Design

Develop role-specific learning paths that integrate NAPSA standards with modern investigation techniques and AI tool proficiency.

  • Investigator core curriculum development
  • Supervisor leadership modules
  • Intake staff screening protocols
  • AI tools integration training design
Phase 3

Platform Development

Build or configure the training delivery platform with blended learning formats—self-paced modules, live workshops, and scenario-based simulations.

  • Learning management system configuration
  • Interactive scenario development
  • Assessment and certification workflows
  • Progress tracking dashboards
Phase 4

Launch & Continuous Learning

Roll out the training academy with train-the-trainer programs and establish ongoing improvement cycles based on performance data.

  • Train-the-trainer certification
  • Phased cohort rollout
  • Feedback loop implementation
  • Quarterly curriculum updates
