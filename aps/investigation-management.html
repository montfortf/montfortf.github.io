The Challenge

Investigations Demand Rigor That Paper Processes Can’t Deliver

APS investigators handle cases involving vulnerable adults where evidence integrity is paramount and multi-agency coordination is essential. Yet most agencies still rely on paper-based evidence collection, manual interview notes, and phone calls to coordinate with law enforcement, healthcare providers, and district attorneys.

The ACL Final Rule requires tiered response based on risk level, standardized investigation procedures, and comprehensive documentation — standards that paper processes and disconnected systems cannot consistently meet. When evidence is lost or procedures are inconsistent, vulnerable adults pay the price.

Without Structured Investigation Tools:

  • ✕ Lost or compromised evidence without chain of custody
  • ✕ Inconsistent investigation procedures across workers
  • ✕ Multi-agency coordination gaps that delay resolution
  • ✕ Tiered response non-compliance risking federal funding

A rigorous methodology that delivers an investigation platform built for the unique demands of APS field work and multi-agency collaboration.

Phase 4
